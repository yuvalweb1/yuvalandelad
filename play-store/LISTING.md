## Data Safety Form — answers

Play Console → Policy → App content → Data safety.

### Does your app collect or share any of the required user data types?

> **No.**

### Is all of the user data collected by your app encrypted in transit?

> **Yes.** All app traffic (loading the app from the web, Google Fonts) is HTTPS.

### Do you provide a way for users to request that their data be deleted?

> **N/A.** No data is collected.

### Are you committed to following the Play Families Policy?

> **No.** App is not directed at children under 13.

### Data types collected — full list

For each category below, the answer is **"Data type not collected"**:

- Personal info (name, email, address, phone, race, etc.)
- Financial info (purchase history, credit card info, etc.) — *In-app subscription billing goes through Google Play Billing. Google handles the payment data, not us. This is exempt from "collected" per Play policy.*
- Health and fitness
- Messages (SMS, email, IM)
- Photos and videos — *The user picks a WhatsApp .zip from their device. We extract photos/voice/videos and process them locally. We do not transmit, store on a server, or share them.*
- Audio files
- Files and docs
- Calendar
- Contacts
- App activity (interactions, search history, etc.)
- Web browsing
- App info and performance (crash logs, diagnostics)
- Device or other IDs

### "Why is your app collecting data?"

> Not applicable. The app does not collect data.

### Account creation / sign-in?

> **No account.** Users use the app anonymously.

### Third-party libraries collecting data?

> Disclose **Google Fonts** (loads fonts on app open). No tracking SDKs.

## Release tracks — recommended order

1. **Internal testing** (you + 5-10 friends): upload the AAB, distribute via
   Play Store internal track. Test the install, IAP flow, share targets,
   parsing, deep links.
2. **Closed testing** (50-100 testers): broader real-world. Iterate.
3. **Open testing** (anyone with the link): pre-launch promotion.
4. **Production**: full Play Store listing live.

Don't skip directly to production for v1 — internal testing catches
99% of crashes before reviewers see them, which keeps your "rejected"
counter clean.
